## Goals

The goals of this policy are to:

- make it clear when software, contracts, docs, or services are official;
- let users truthfully refer to the project;
- prevent confusing forks, marketplace packs, services, or integrations from implying endorsement; and
- keep the name useful for the community.

## Allowed uses

You may use the json-contracts name without separate permission when you are:

- referring to the official project truthfully, such as "compatible with json-contracts";
- linking to the official project;
- describing that your software uses, integrates with, or is based on json-contracts;
- preserving notices required by the project licenses;
- using the name in package metadata, docs, blog posts, videos, talks, or tutorials in a way that is not misleading; or
- making non-commercial community references that do not imply official status.

Examples of acceptable wording:

- "Works with json-contracts."
- "A contract pack for json-contracts."
- "Forked from json-contracts."
- "Powered by json-contracts."

You need prior written permission from Harry Giunta to use the json-contracts marks in a way that suggests official status, ownership, endorsement, certification, or sponsorship.

Examples that need permission include:

- using names such as "JSON Contracts Cloud", "Official json-contracts Marketplace", or "json-contracts Certified";

## Forks and modified versions

You may fork and modify the code under its copyright license, but if you distribute a modified version you must avoid confusing users about whether it is the official project.

Recommended naming examples:

- "Acme JSON Contracts, based on json-contracts"
- "Acme fork of json-contracts"
- "json-contracts-compatible contract tools"

Avoid names that imply the fork is the official project or successor unless you have written permission.

## Contract packs and marketplace packs

Official starter contracts distributed by this repository may refer to json-contracts as part of the official project.

Third-party or marketplace contract packs may say they are "for json-contracts" or "compatible with json-contracts", but they must not imply they are official, certified, endorsed, or maintained by Harry Giunta unless they have written permission.

Third-party packs should use their own distinctive pack names and should include their own license file or license metadata.

## Logos and visual identity

If project logos or visual assets are published, do not use them as the primary branding for your own product, service, pack, website, or company without permission. Do not modify them in a way that implies official endorsement.

To request trademark permission or report confusing use, contact the project owner, Harry Giunta, through the official project repository or the contact channel published for the project.

Harry Giunta may update this policy over time. Existing permissions may be reviewed if use becomes misleading, harmful, or inconsistent with this policy.
